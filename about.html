About

Senior at Brown University · Electrical Engineering & International and Public Affairs

I am a senior at Brown University pursuing dual degrees in Electrical Engineering and International and Public Affairs. My work sits at the boundary of how transformative technologies are built and how they are governed — with a particular focus on quantum computing.

On the technical side, I am drawn to the physics and hardware underlying quantum systems: how we engineer devices at the atomic scale, characterize their properties, and push toward fault-tolerant computation. On the policy side, I am interested in how governments and institutions are beginning to reckon with a technology that will affect cryptography, national security, and scientific capability simultaneously.

I have contributed to Monte Carlo neutron transport simulations as part of a DOE-funded exascale computing project, and I have written on energy regulation, defense technology, and space policy for the Brown Political Review.

I am currently looking for PhD programs and full-time positions in hardware development. If you work on quantum systems, materials science, or the policy landscape around emerging hardware, I would enjoy talking.

Outside the lab

Both reward patience and careful measurement, which are not entirely different from what good experimental work demands.
